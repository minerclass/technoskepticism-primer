# Technoskepticism &mdash; A Primer

A small, self-contained companion page that defines **technoskepticism** and walks through the **five technoskeptical questions**, their roots in Neil Postman, and a **pedagogical-friction** overlay. Built as a reusable, citable anchor to link from the AAACS 2026 presentation, the dissertation proposal, and related writing.

## Live page

<https://minerclass.github.io/technoskepticism-primer/>

## What it covers

- **What technoskepticism is** &mdash; disciplined questioning, not refusal.
- **The five technoskeptical questions** &mdash; adapted by Dan Krutka and Scott Metzger from Postman, via the Civics of Technology curriculum. Each is paired with its Postman root, a generative-AI classroom prompt, and a pedagogical-friction overlay.
- **Provenance** &mdash; a lineage table mapping Postman's five propositions to the five questions.
- **The friction overlay** &mdash; productive vs. exclusionary friction, and why the curriculum conversation should not be auto-completed.
- **Sources and how to cite.**

## Open locally

Open `index.html` in any modern browser. No build step, no dependencies.
